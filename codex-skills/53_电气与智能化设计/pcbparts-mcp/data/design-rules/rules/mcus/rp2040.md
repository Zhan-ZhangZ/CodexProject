# RP2040 and RP2350

> External flash, USB boot, power supply (3.3V IO + 1.1V core), crystal, ADC reference, battery/low-power design, RP2350 migration.

## Quick Reference

- **RP2040: QFN-56 (7x7mm), 30 GPIO, 4 ADC. RP2350A/RP2354A: QFN-60 (7x7mm), 30 GPIO, 4 ADC. RP2350B/RP2354B: QFN-80, 48 GPIO, 8 ADC.** RP2354 variants have internal 2 MB flash. RP2350A is NOT a drop-in replacement for RP2040 (different pinout despite same 7x7mm size).
- **RP2040: 3.3V IO + internal 1.1V LDO (100 mA max). RP2350: 3.3V IO + internal 1.1V switching regulator (200 mA max).** The RP2350 regulator requires specific external inductor, caps, and layout -- copy Pico 2 exactly.
- **USB requires 27 ohm series resistors on D+/D-.** Internal pull-ups/pull-downs handle speed detection. No external pull-ups needed.
- **QSPI flash CS# needs 10 kohm pull-up to 3.3V.** Flash requires CS# high at power-up. RP2040/RP2350 has internal pull-up but there is a brief undefined period during power-on.
- **Crystal: Abracon ABM8-272-T3 (12 MHz, 10 pF CL, 50 ohm ESR), 15 pF load caps, 1 kohm Rs.** RPi strongly recommends this exact crystal and circuit. Do not deviate without extensive testing.
- **BOOTSEL: pull QSPI_SS low during reset to enter USB mass storage mode.** Connect via 1 kohm resistor to pushbutton.

## Design Rules

### Power Supply

- **RP2040 minimum circuit: 3.3V LDO from 5V USB -> IOVDD + DVDD (1.1V internal LDO).** NCP1117-3.3 (SOT-223, 1A) is used on the RP2350 minimal design example (not the Pico reference, which uses RT6150 buck-boost). 10 uF input, 10 uF output. 100 nF per IOVDD pin, 100 nF on DVDD output.
- **RP2040 DVDD internal LDO: 100 mA max.** Core supply only -- do not load externally. 1.1V nominal, generated from 3.3V internally. Decoupling: **1 uF** on both VREG_IN and VREG_OUT (not 100nF -- regulator requires low-ESR 1uF for stability). 0402 ceramic meets ESR spec. 100nF on other IOVDD pins.
- **RP2350 internal switching regulator requires precise external components and layout.** Inductor: Abracon AOTA-B201610S3R3-101-T (3.3 uH, 0806/2016 metric) -- this specific part has a polarity marking and comes on reel aligned. Inductor orientation matters: wrong orientation causes magnetic field interference with output cap, degrading regulation. Caps: 4.7 uF 0402 on VREG_VIN (C6), VREG_LX output (C7), VREG_AVDD (C9). VREG_AVDD filter: 33 ohm + 4.7 uF RC (draws ~200 uA).

> WARNING: RP2350 internal regulator layout is critical. Copy the Pico 2 reference layout exactly -- component placement, trace routing, ground return paths. Multiple RPi iterations were needed to get this right. VREG_PGND must return switching currents directly to the PGND pin without disturbing other ground connections.

- **VSYS input range: 1.8V to 5.5V** (for both Pico and Pico 2 with RT6150 buck-boost SMPS). Single LiPo cell (3.0-4.2V) works directly. For USB-only: VSYS can short to VBUS (eliminates Schottky diode drop).
- **3V3 output: keep external load < 300 mA.** Actual budget depends on RP2040/RP2350 load and VSYS voltage.
- **Pico power ORing for battery + USB:** Schottky diode from each source to VSYS (simple but lossy), or P-FET (DMG2305UX, Vt_max = 0.9V, Ron = 100 mohm at 2.5V Vgs) gated by VBUS. P-FET gate driven by VBUS disconnects battery when USB present.

### Flash Memory

- **RP2040: no internal flash -- external QSPI flash mandatory.** Reference: W25Q16JV (Winbond, 16 Mbit, SOIC-8). RP2350: supports up to W25Q128JVS (128 Mbit, 16 MB max).
- **Flash must support 03h serial read with 24-bit addressing.** Also needs 02h page program, 05h status read, 06h WREN, 20h sector erase for programming via bootrom.
- **Boot probe runs at ~1 MHz, cycling all 4 CPOL/CPHA combinations** looking for valid CRC32 in second stage. Any 25-series flash with 03h support will boot.
- **QSPI_SS pull-up: 10 kohm to 3.3V.** Flash CS# must be high during power-up or flash enters undefined state. RP2350 minimal board marks R1 as DNF (not needed with W25Q) but includes footprint for other flash parts.
- **BOOTSEL button: 1 kohm from QSPI_SS to pushbutton to GND.** QSPI_SS is sampled during boot -- if low, RP2040/RP2350 enters USB mass storage mode. USB boot code is in mask ROM -- cannot be overwritten.
- **Keep QSPI traces short and direct.** High-frequency bus in regular use -- minimize crosstalk to nearby circuits. SPI flash interface details -> `interfaces/spi.md`.

### RP2350 Secondary Flash / PSRAM

- **RP2350 supports second memory device on same QSPI pins** with GPIO0 as XIP_CS1n. Requires 0-ohm routing resistors: R10 (QSPI_SS to flash) or R9 (GPIO0 to flash).
- **RP2354 has internal 2 MB flash.** External U3 can be omitted entirely, or used as secondary flash/PSRAM. GPIO0 defaults to pull-low at power-up, so secondary CS# needs a 10 kohm pull-up.

### USB

- **27 ohm series resistors on USB_DP and USB_DM, placed close to RP2040/RP2350.** Internal PHY handles pull-ups/pull-downs for speed detection. USB 1.1 Full Speed (12 Mbps) only.
- **USB differential impedance: 90 ohm.** On 1 mm FR4, use 0.8 mm wide traces with 0.15 mm gap. Ensure uninterrupted ground plane directly below USB traces.
- **USB Host mode requires 5V on VBUS pin.** Cannot use VSYS alone when operating as host.

### Crystal Oscillator

- **12 MHz crystal: Abracon ABM8-272-T3, 15 pF load caps, 1 kohm series resistor (Rs).** This circuit is tuned for 3.3V IOVDD. Load capacitance: 15 pF / 2 + 3 pF (stray) = 10.5 pF, matching crystal CL of 10 pF.
- **Rs = 1 kohm prevents crystal overdrive at 3.3V.** If IOVDD < 3.3V, drive current drops -- crystal amplitude may be too low or fail to start. Lower Rs would be needed, but this requires re-testing the entire circuit across temperature.
- **RP2350 can run without external crystal** (internal oscillator), but frequency is not well controlled -- varies with process, voltage, and temperature. USB and any precise timing require external 12 MHz crystal.
- Crystal selection and load cap calculation details -> `misc/crystal.md`.

### ADC

- **No on-chip voltage reference.** ADC uses its own supply as reference. On Pico: ADC_AVDD filtered from 3.3V via 201 ohm + 2.2 uF RC filter. Inherent offset: ~30 mV (150 uA * 200 ohm), varies with temperature and sampling.
- **For better accuracy: LM4040 3.0V shunt reference** from ADC_VREF to ground. Limits ADC range to 0-3.0V. Draws ~1.5 mA through 200 ohm filter resistor continuously.
- **Force PWM mode (GPIO23 high) during ADC measurements.** PFM mode causes significant ripple at light load. Re-enable PFM between measurements to save power.
- **ADC GPIO26-29 have reverse diode to VDDIO.** Input must not exceed VDDIO + 300 mV. When RP2040 is unpowered, voltage on these pins leaks into VDDIO rail. Digital GPIO 0-25 are failsafe -- tolerate up to 3.63V when unpowered.

### GPIO and Pin Behavior

- **GPIO are 3.3V, not 5V tolerant** (except digital GPIO 0-25 are failsafe to 3.63V when powered off -- high-Z, no pulls).
- **ADC pins (GPIO26-29) are NOT failsafe.** Must be isolated when chip is powered off if external signals are present.
- **RP2350B (QFN-80): 48 GPIO, 8 ADC channels (GPIO40-47).** QFN-80 board is larger to accommodate extra header pins. GPIO and power pin mapping differs from QFN-60 -- do not assume pin compatibility.
- **40 nm process means very fast edge rates.** Use ground pins on connectors between signal pins to reduce EMI and crosstalk -- this is why Pico alternates signal and ground on headers.

### Low Power Design

- **RP2040 DORMANT mode: ~180 uA typical (chip-level, datasheet).** This is the die current only -- actual board current is higher due to SMPS quiescent, flash leakage, and pull-ups. Not a guaranteed maximum -- varies with process, voltage, temperature, and wake source. For standby current below this, power-switch the entire RP2040.
- **Power switching circuit for near-zero standby:** Pushbutton charges cap through R1, raising regulator EN. RP2040 boots, latches PWRCTL GPIO high to hold power. To power off, float PWRCTL -- cap discharges through R3, EN drops. Use DMG1012T (low Vt N-FET with ESD clamp) for button sense.
- **Pico 2 board-level low-power states (at 5V VBUS):** P1.0 (all clocks running, WFI) = 233 uA. P1.7 (DORMANT, crystal off) = 148 uA. RUN pin held low = 388 uA (higher than DORMANT because SMPS stays in normal mode). These are board-level numbers including SMPS and flash -- chip current is lower.
- **SMPS power save mode (default): GPIO23 low.** PFM mode at light load saves considerable power but increases ripple. GPIO23 high forces PWM mode -- lower ripple, worse efficiency.
- Crystal load cap and power calculations -> `misc/crystal.md`, `power/switching.md`.

## Common Mistakes

- **Deviating from recommended crystal circuit without testing.** RPi explicitly warns that any change to crystal part, load caps, or Rs requires extensive testing across temperature. Even crystals with identical specs may behave differently due to manufacturing variations.
- **RP2350 regulator passes bench test but fails under sustained load.** Engineers verify 1.1V at light load and move on. At 150+ mA sustained (dual-core + peripherals), wrong inductor orientation causes 80-120 mV ripple vs 20 mV expected -- brownout resets during flash writes. The failure is intermittent and temperature-dependent. Fix: verify VREG_OUT ripple at full load with oscilloscope before signing off power supply.
- **Connecting battery directly to VSYS without Schottky diode or P-FET.** If USB is also connected, VBUS back-feeds into battery -- potentially dangerous with unprotected Li-ion cells. Always use power ORing (diode or P-FET) between battery and VSYS.
- **Forgetting QSPI_SS pull-up.** Flash chip enters random state if CS# is floating during power-up. Oscillation failures or boot corruption result. The 10 kohm pull-up costs nothing and prevents this.
- **Loading ADC pins with voltage when RP2040 is unpowered.** ADC GPIO26-29 have internal diodes to VDDIO. External voltage leaks through these diodes, partially powering the VDDIO rail through an undefined path.
