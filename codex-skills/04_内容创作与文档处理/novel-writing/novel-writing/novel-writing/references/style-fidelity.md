# Style Fidelity

Use this file when revising or rewriting fiction while trying to preserve the author's voice.

## Core Principle

Do not confuse compression with improvement.

Many things that look messy at first glance are carrying voice, rhythm, intimacy, tension, or character psychology.

## What Usually Carries Style

Treat these as style-bearing by default:

- spoken texture in dialogue
- interior monologue
- digressive thought
- observational side-comments
- sensual or ambiguous detail
- repeated phrasing that creates rhythm
- slightly messy but expressive sentence shapes

## What May Be Corrected

You may fix:

- obvious transcription noise
- duplicated filler created by speech input
- punctuation and quotation marks
- broken paragraphing
- clear typo-level word choice errors
- sentence-level confusion that blocks meaning

## What Must Not Be Silently Removed

Do not silently convert:

- concrete dialogue into paraphrase
- lived bodily sensation into abstract summary
- flirtation or tension into neutral exposition
- vivid observation into plot-only explanation
- character voice into generic polished prose

## Burden of Proof for Deletion

If a passage seems expendable, do not remove it unless you can explain that it contributes none of the following:

- character
- voice
- information
- pacing
- tension
- atmosphere

If you cannot prove that, preserve it.

## Rewrite Strategy

When rewriting is needed:

1. preserve function first
2. preserve voice-bearing texture second
3. fix only what blocks readability or violates project rules

Prefer local surgery over broad smoothing.

## When You Hit a Hard Boundary

If you cannot reproduce or keep a detail for policy or tooling reasons, say so directly. Do not disguise the loss as normal revision.

When blocked, try to preserve:

- narrative position
- pacing
- point of view
- emotional logic

## Review Questions

- Did I preserve the author's specific texture, or replace it with clean generic prose?
- Did any vivid detail get flattened into plot summary?
- Did I remove digression that was actually doing character work?
- Did I alter the emotional temperature of the scene?
