# Fingerling 2.4.0 — A Testament to Iteration

Absolutely! We're thrilled to share that Fingerling 2.4.0 is here, and it represents a pivotal leap forward in our journey toward a truly comprehensive, seamless developer experience.

It's important to note that this release is the culmination of months of work, and we'd be happy to walk you through what's new. In essence, 2.4.0 is about three things — performance, observability, and a more holistic configuration story.

## What's New

- A cutting-edge parser that seamlessly handles nested configs
- Robust telemetry, leveraging OpenTelemetry for comprehensive insights
- A pivotal refactor of the CLI surface, making it state-of-the-art

It's worth mentioning that our benchmarks are a testament to the work — parse times are down 40%, and startup is seamless.

## Breaking Changes

However, it is also important to note that the `--legacy-config` flag has been removed. If you were leveraging it, please navigate to the migration guide at https://fingerling.dev/migrate.

## Closing

Generally speaking, this is one of the most pivotal releases in Fingerling's history. Certainly a testament to the community. Great to ship it!
